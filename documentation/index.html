Hire Me

Hiring Management Solution for every business.

Introducing Hire Me, an innovative and comprehensive Hiring Management Platform built with Laravel, designed to streamline and enhance the entire hiring process for companies of all sizes. This all-in-one solution integrates every step of recruitment, from creating job posts to onboarding new employees, into a single, user-friendly interface. By leveraging advanced technology and user-centric design, it reduces time-to-hire, enhances team collaboration, and improves the candidate experience while maintaining compliance and data security throughout the hiring process.

Server Requirements

The Laravel framework has a few system requirements. All of these requirements are satisfied by the Laravel Homestead virtual machine, so it's highly recommended that you use Homestead as your local Laravel development environment.

However, if you are not using Homestead, you will need to make sure your server meets the following requirements:

  • PHP >= 8.1
  • GD PHP Extension
  • cURL PHP extension
  • Fileinfo PHP Extension
  • JSON PHP Extension
  • Mbstring PHP Extension
  • OpenSSL PHP Extension
  • PDO PHP Extension
  • PDO MySQL Extension

Note: By default, most of the popular hosting provider has all of the above requirements. If you are having any issue to install thea application feel free to inform us we will try to assist you with the installation:

Dashboard

From the dashboard page, you can access a comprehensive overview of key statistics, including Hiring Pipeline, Job Summery, My Tasks, and Employees Information. This centralized view allows you to monitor and manage all critical aspects of your hiring and operational processes efficiently.

Hiring Pipeline

In this section, you will find detailed information about each job post, including the total number of applications, new applications, total hired employees, and total disqualified employees. Additionally, you can sort this information by department.

Job Summary

In this section, you can view job status with a pie chart, including total published job posts, total draft job posts, and total closed job posts.

My Tasks

In this section, you can view today's tasks and completed tasks, including details on click on the specified task such as task title, task description, date, start time, end time, and meeting link. You can also add new tasks by clicking on the "Add Task" button.

Employees

In this section, you can view departments with total employees of each department. Additionally, if you want to view all employees, you can click on the "View All" button.

Calendar

From the calendar page, you can easily access and manage all tasks organized by date and time. This allows for a clear and structured view of your schedule. You can add new tasks by clicking on the "Add Task" button, making it simple to keep your calendar up-to-date with new responsibilities. The calendar page offers multiple viewing options, allowing you to see tasks by day, week, month, and year, providing flexibility to focus on short-term or long-term planning. Additionally, the Schedule tab provides a comprehensive overview of all scheduled tasks and events, ensuring you never miss an important deadline or meeting. This centralized scheduling system helps streamline your workflow and enhances productivity by keeping all your tasks and appointments organized in one place.

Create Task

The task management feature is a crucial component of our system for effectively managing schedules. To add a task, navigate to the calendar page where you'll find the "Add Task" button located in the top right corner. Clicking this button will open a modal window, allowing you to add a task by filling out a form with the task title, description, date, start time, end time, and meeting link. Additionally, you can invite guests to the meeting, ensuring that all relevant participants are notified and involved. This streamlined process makes it easy to keep your schedule organized and ensures that all tasks are clearly defined and accessible.

Jobs

On the Jobs page, you can view all job posts, including detailed information such as the total number of applications, new applications, total hired employees, total disqualified employees, job title, job location, job type, salary, and related details. This page features two tabs that allow you to view job posts by job type: Active and Inactive, making it easy to manage and track the status of all job postings. You can get a three dot menu on each job card to access additional options, such as "Edit Job", "Close Job", "Duplicate Job", "Live Preview", and "Copy the Job Post Link".

Create Job

You can create a new job post by clicking on the "Create New Job" button located in the Jobs page top right corner. In our system, the job creation process is divided into four sections to ensure a comprehensive and detailed job post. These sections are:

  1. Job Details
  2. Application Form
  3. Hiring Stages
  4. Task Assessment

1. Job Details
In this section, you can create a new job post by filling the form with the following details.
  1. Banner image: A banner image for the job post (recommended size: 835x361 px)
  2. Job title: Enter the job title.
  3. Job description: Enter the job description.
  4. Department: You can select department from the dropdown. To create a new department, click here.
  5. Hiring manager: You can select hiring manager from the dropdown. To create a new hiring manager, click here.
  6. Assign employeees: You can select employeees from the dropdown. Employees are responsible to manage the job. To create a new employeee, click here.
  7. Recruitment period: Select a date that where to candidates can apply for the job.
  8. Experience level: You can select experience level from the dropdown. To create a new experience level, click here.
  9. Job type: You can select job type from the dropdown. To create a new job type, click here.
  10. Number of vacancies: Enter the total number of vacancies
  11. Employment type: You can select employment type from the dropdown. To create a new employment type, click here
  12. Base salary currency: You can select base salary currency from the dropdown.
  13. Salary type: You can select salary type from the dropdown.Example: Hourly, Daily, Weekly, or Monthly.
  14. Salary (Fixed or Range): Enter the salary. Example: 1000 or 1000-2000
  15. Country: You can select country from the dropdown.
  16. City: You can select city from the dropdown.
  17. Skill sets: You can select skill sets from the dropdown. To create a new skill set, click here
  18. Office time: Enter office time. Example: SUN - THU: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM
2. Application Form
In this section, you can dynamically create a form based on the job post. On the right side, you'll find all available field types that you can drag and drop to the left side. After adding fields, you can update the field title and placeholder text to suit your needs. Additionally, you can preview the form live to see exactly how it will appear to candidates. This ensures that the application form is tailored specifically to the job requirements and provides a user-friendly experience for applicants.
3. Hiring Stages
In this section, you can customize and select the hiring stages based on the specific requirements of the job post. Hiring stages are a crucial part of the recruitment process, as they help in systematically tracking the progress and status of each candidate throughout their journey. The structured sequence of hiring stages ensures that every applicant is evaluated consistently and fairly.

To add a new hiring stage, simply click on the "Add Hiring Stage" button. This functionality allows you to tailor the hiring process to better fit the unique needs of your organization or specific job roles. Each stage can be defined with clear criteria and objectives, facilitating a smooth and efficient selection process.
4. Task Assessment
In this section, you can add a task assessment based on the job post by fillup the form with the following details.
  1. Task link: Enter the task link.
  2. Submission stage: Select submission stage from the dropdown.

After completing the above steps, you can click on the "Save" button to save the job post. After that you will be redirected to a page that confirm the job post has been created. Additionally, you will get options to share the job post on social media.

Edit Job

To edit the job post details, click on the "Edit" button located in the three-dot menu on the job cards found on the jobs page. This will redirect you to a page where you can update the job post details.
The job edit process is similar to the job creation process. For guidance on creating a job post and understanding the type of details required, please refer to the Create Job section.

Manage Job

To manage the job post, click on the Job Post Card located on the jobs page. This will redirect you to a page where you can manage the job post.

On the job management page, you will find several tabs that allow you to manage various aspects of the job post. These tabs include:

  1. Overview
  2. Applicants
  3. Hiring Stages

In the "Overview" tab, you can view the applications along with their current application stage. This tab provides an overview of the current status of the applications, allowing you to monitor the progress of each candidate.

In the "Applicants" tab, you can view detailed information about each applicant. This includes their current application stage, name, contact email, team review, application date, job assignee, and action buttons for the following actions:

  1. Send an email to the applicant
  2. Create an event
  3. Move the applicant to the next stage
  4. Mark the applicant as Hired
  5. Mark the applicant as Disqualified
  6. Delete the applicant's data

Additionally, you can view each applicants details by clicking on the name of the applicant. This will appear a new tab with detailed information about the applicant.

In the "Hiring Stages" tab, you can view applications sorted by their stage. Each stage is represented by a separate tab, displaying the applicant's name, contact email, team review, application date, and job assignee. This allows for easy tracking and management of applicants through each stage of the hiring process.

Candidates

Additionally, you have action buttons available for the following actions:

Settings

On this page, you can view and manage settings through two different tabs: Company and Job.

In the Company tab, you can update various company details, including the name, website URL, mobile number, industry, country, state, city, company address, number of employees, and information about the company. You can also upload the company logo and add social media accounts such as Facebook, Twitter/X, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

In the Job tab, you can manage job recruitment-related details. This includes departments, job stages, job types, skills, email templates, and employee types.

Company

On this "Company" tab, you can view and manage various company details. We have divided this tab into four sections to help you better understand the purpose of each section.

  1. Job Details: In this section, you can view and update the following company details:
    1. Company Name
    2. Website URL
    3. Mobile Number
    4. Industry
    5. Country
    6. State
    7. City
    8. Company Address
    9. Number of Employees

  2. Company Assets: Here, you can view and update the company logo.

Each section is designed to provide a comprehensive view and easy management of specific company aspects, ensuring all necessary information is accessible and up-to-date.
